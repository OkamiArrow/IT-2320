The first anime that I had ever watched with my little brothers was probably Naruto. It was the easiest anime that we could talk about and relate with. We would sit up for hours and wait for the newest episode to come out. I would always go to the store and buy the new manga when it was out so we could read it together and talk about the story and who our favorite characters were. Some of the closer moments my brother and I still have to this day is when we go out in the backyard and grab our kunai and throwing knives and compete to see who can hit the mark the best on a tree or bget the most to stay in the tree. Over the summer we actually set a can on a string and after we hit the can we moved on to see who could cut the fishing wire from far away. Needless to say, we should just stick to watching it on the anime.




T h e  N e x t  P h a s e


As we got older, obviously the shows we were used to watching were ending one by one and we needed to find new ones to watch. But as we grew and changed, so did our taste in what we liked in an anime. Of course we could always go back and watch old episodes of other shows but we were starting to like different shows because of our age gap and that was okay. It made us have to talk about and debate about what we liked and disliked about our new favorites like SteinsGate and Rick And Morty. It was another reason to talk. If it wasn't for these shows my brothers and I might not have gotten so close. They gave us a way to close the age gap that wasn't available before. So for that reason I will always have a great deal of respect and admiration for anime. Below are some animes that I would advise if someone were to ask about what they should watch for a starter anime.



  1. Bleach
    • An action-filled show about fighting in the death realm
  2. RWBY
    • A show about four characters using the power of "Dust" to protect their world from monsters.
  3. Full Metal Alchemist
    • Two brothers dabble in scientific magic to try and revive their mother and end up losing a lot more...
  4. Samurai Champloo
    • Filled with modern-day samurai and a lot of fighting and comedy.
  5. Mushishi
    • A nice calm show about a man who helps spirits move on to the afterlife.
  6. XXXHolic
    • Also a show about helping spirits but not always helping in the way that you'd expect.
  7. DRRRR!!!
    • The headless horseman rides a motorcylce in this modern-day anime adaptation of monsters and gangs.
